Slide 9

What is AI?

Speaker Notes

Then, when it's ready and they release it to the public, you send it a request. And it generates responses one word at a time. For each word, it then thinks, 'Based on all the patterns I’ve learned in training and the current conversation I’m having, what word comes next?' It’s choosing the word it thinks should come next based on the patterns that it learned in its training. And it does that for the that word, and the next word, and the next word, until it feels that the answer is complete.

02 When should I use ai?

Speaker Notes

AI is a powerful tool for creativity, summarization, and streamlining repetitive tasks. But it’s not designed to replace experts in high-stakes areas like law, medicine, or finance.
Now people are using AI for all of these already. I am not telling you never to do it. I am telling you to do it carefully. Get a second opinion. Verify the specifics. Do not replace a real expert when the stakes are high. And remember, there are moments where a human touch is valued, like wedding vows or a sympathy note.

Slide 40

Tip: Ask Me Questions

Speaker Notes

I’m going to start with one of my favorite tips: And it’s: Add “ask me one question at a time, until you have enough information to give me a great answer” to the end of your prompts. Instead of guessing what you need, the AI will ask you! I use this a lot. I like this because the AI can use my last answer to think of new questions. Remember, it uses the full context of the entire conversation to decide what word should come next in its responses.

Slide 41

Tip: Help Me Create a Prompt

Speaker Notes

Unsure how to write your prompt? Ask for help. Include “Help me create a prompt for this,” and it will create a structured prompt. It's like having a prompt coach built right in! I use this alot for deep research when the response sometimes take up to 60 minutes to complete.

Tip: Match Your Tone

Speaker Notes

The next tip is tone matching. This is a technique to have the AI’s response style match your writing style. Give the model some examples of text you have already created. This could be emails you have written, documents, even text messages. Then ask it to rewrite its response, matching your tone and style.

Slide 53

Beyond Prompting

Speaker Notes

One way to improve results for complex projects is to use a second model to challenge the first. One model like ChatGPT generates the response, then a different model like Claude critiques it - and you loop until the output improves.
Different models have different strengths, and the disagreement is the point. That’s what exposes gaps and leads to better results.

Slide 54

Beyond Prompting

Speaker Notes

Here’s how to do that:
You ask the second model to critique it - look for what’s missing, what’s unclear, or what could be improved.
Then feed that critique back into the first model to have it revise the response.
You’re not just prompting anymore, you’re orchestrating a system.

Slide 55

Beyond Prompting

Speaker Notes

And the last thing Ill touch on is that today, even with AI, you’re still doing the orchestration.You break the work into steps, prompt each one, and connect it all together.
Agents change that.

Slide 56

Beyond Prompting

Speaker Notes

With an agent, you describe the outcome you want, and the AI can plan the steps, work across different tools, and take action within limits you set. You are still responsible for the judgment and approvals, but the AI starts moving from advisor to operator.

Slide 57

Beyond Prompting

Speaker Notes

For an example, Let's say you want to go on a vacation to Santa Barbara. We can just assign the outcome: plan and book a 3-day trip.

Slide 58

Beyond Prompting

Speaker Notes

The agent figures out its own steps. It looks at flights, hotels, activities, checks availability, compares options, and builds a plan.
But it’s not just suggesting things, it’s actually doing them. Visiting sites, pulling real-time data, checking your calendar, making sure things fit. When it needs input, it asks. When it needs to spend money, it can pause for approval.But for low-risk actions, like making a dinner reservation, it can just go ahead and do it.
