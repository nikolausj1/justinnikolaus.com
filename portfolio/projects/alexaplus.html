Next-Gen Conversational AI

Company Amazon
Role Design Lead
Responsibility Multimodal conversation design for AI agent interactions
Year 2024–2025

Overview

As AI agents become more capable, existing interaction models break down. I led design for a next-generation conversational AI experience, defining how voice and visual information coordinate dynamically across multi-turn agent conversations on multimodal devices. This was greenfield work with no market precedent. No existing product had solved how to orchestrate rich visual context alongside voice in real-time agent interactions.

The Problem

Voice assistants handle simple requests well, but as agents gain the ability to perform complex, multi-turn tasks, the traditional conversation model fails. When an agent is booking travel, researching products, or managing schedules, it needs to present visual information that helps users make decisions, not just respond to commands. The challenge was determining what to show, when to show it, how it should evolve across turns, and how to prevent cognitive overload.

My Approach

I led the design direction for how the conversation experience would work across voice and visual channels. I managed a design team and drove vision, strategy, and cross-functional documentation for product and engineering partners.

I began by classifying different types of conversations users have with AI agents, analyzing the structure of multi-turn dialogues across domains like travel, shopping, entertainment, and productivity. Through this research, I identified common information patterns that recur across conversation types, which helped define when users need rich visual context versus a simple spoken response.

A critical early challenge was that the team had no aligned interaction model for how conversational content should appear and behave on screen. I explored multiple conversational CX models, developing and documenting each with core principles, visual examples, and tradeoff analyses. I then drove a comprehensive cross-functional design review that achieved full alignment across design, product, and engineering leadership. The chosen direction became the foundational interaction pattern for the project, unblocking subsequent work across teams.

The Conversation Framework

Beyond defining visual behavior, I recognized the need for a structural foundation that would align how the entire organization thought about multimodal agent conversations. I authored a Conversation Framework, a classification system for multimodal agent conversations designed to provide systematic rigor for guiding design decisions and AI behavior at scale.

The framework established a shared language across design, engineering, and product management for coordinating voice and visual responses. It became the authoritative reference for the project, ensuring that every team was working from the same conceptual foundation when designing how the agent communicated across modalities.

Impact

The work established foundational principles for how multimodal AI agents should coordinate voice and visual information. The interaction model and conversation framework I developed influenced thinking across the organization and demonstrated that agentic AI experiences require fundamentally new interaction patterns, not incremental improvements to existing assistant designs.
