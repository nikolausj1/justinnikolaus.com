Animated Wizard Card

Company Personal Project
Role Designer & Maker
Responsibility Hardware, Software, Design
Year 2026

Overview

My kids were so excited to visit Honeydukes at the Wizarding World in Universal Studios Florida and get their hands on some Chocolate Frogs. They loved the tins and the wizard cards inside, which had a cool effect using lenticular printing. But I wanted to make them better. More realistic. Moving portraits, just like in the films. So I built one.

This is a real Chocolate Frog tin with an animated wizard portrait inside. When you open the lid, a random wizard comes to life on a tiny screen embedded in the card. Close the lid and it goes dark, ready to surprise you with a different wizard next time. Dumbledore, the four Hogwarts founders -- they're all in there.

How It Works

A magnetic reed switch mounted near the lid edge detects a small neodymium magnet glued inside the lid. When the lid opens, the magnet separates from the switch, the Pi wakes the display and plays a randomly selected wizard animation. When the lid closes, the magnet triggers the switch again and the screen clears to black. A printed cardboard mat with a portrait-shaped cutout frames the LCD to look like a real Chocolate Frog card from the films.

Hardware

The component stack fits entirely inside a standard Chocolate Frog tin with no modifications to the enclosure: a Raspberry Pi Zero 2 WH drives a 2.4-inch Waveshare LCD via SPI, powered by a PiSugar 3 rechargeable battery so there are no wires. No soldering required. The Pi has pre-soldered headers, the PiSugar connects via pogo pins, and the LCD uses dupont wires secured with hot glue.

Result

The wizard animations were created with Kling AI from still portrait images. Each animation is 8 seconds at 24fps, played to the framebuffer via ffmpeg. The full build guide, source code, printable card templates, and Kling prompts are all available on GitHub.
