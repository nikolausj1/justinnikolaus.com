CrowdCam

Company CrowdCam (Startup)
Role Co-founder
Responsibility End-to-end product design, startup leadership
Year 2015

Overview

I was getting married and needed a way to collect all the photos my friends and family would take and display them live so everyone could enjoy them, including my grandma who wasn't going to download an app. Nothing on the market fit, so I built it myself. What started as a personal project became CrowdCam: a real-time photo display platform for events.

Research

I gathered user insights through surveys, interviews at events, and conversations at wedding shows and conferences. The research revealed clear patterns around what guests and hosts needed, which directly shaped the product's simplicity-first direction.

Design

The guest experience had to be dead simple to succeed. Guests supply the photos, so I eliminated everything unnecessary: no username, no password, no email. It was tempting to collect email addresses so hosts could share the photo album afterward, but I prioritized simplicity over data capture. The result was a two-step flow: enter the event code, post photos.

Impact

CrowdCam was a hit at my wedding. Guests loved it so much they requested it for their own events, turning a personal project into a business. Thousands of users enjoyed it at weddings, corporate events, and concerts around the world. The following summer, CrowdCam was featured at a Weezer concert in Minneapolis.
