Emotionally Aware AI

Company Amazon
Role Concept, Design & Prototype Lead
Responsibility AI prototyping, emerging technology research
Year 2024

Overview

Voice assistants respond to words, but they often miss the emotional and situational subtext. The same phrase can be sincere, sarcastic, or distressed depending on tone, expression, and environment. When the agent ignores that context, responses can feel awkward, insensitive, or tone-deaf. I led an incubation project exploring how a voice agent could respond more appropriately by combining what a user says with nonverbal and situational signals. This was internal work, so I can share my approach and thinking here but not the implementation.

Design Thinking

The core design challenge was defining when and how an agent should act on emotional context. The questions I wrestled with:

  • How should an agent respond when it detects someone is upset but they haven't explicitly said so?
  • How assertive should it be? At what point does emotional awareness cross into feeling surveillant?
  • What happens when signals conflict, like positive words paired with negative tone?
  • How do you keep responses empathetic without being intrusive or presumptuous?

I designed a set of principles to guide the system's behavior. The agent should be conservative when confidence is low, falling back to neutral rather than guessing. When multiple signals agree with high confidence, the agent can adapt its tone and pacing. When signals conflict, the agent should acknowledge ambiguity rather than assume one signal is correct. The goal was an experience that feels natural and attentive, not one that feels like it's analyzing you.

Scope

I owned this project from concept through working prototype. I defined the interaction model, designed the response framework, created end-to-end flows covering how the agent should behave across a range of scenarios, and shaped the demo narrative. I worked with a design technologist to build the environment and connect services. The working prototype was demonstrated live to a curated audience of senior leaders and distinguished engineers across the company.

Impact

Leadership responded strongly to the prototype, with alignment that this direction represents a credible path forward for voice interaction. Multiple teams have since used the interaction framework and design principles from this work as a reference point for their own products.
