Target Store Mode

Company Target
Role Sr. User Experience Architect
Responsibility Mobile UX strategy, native app development
Year 2011

Overview

Target had a well-liked flagship iPhone app for online orders. With 60% of shoppers using mobile phones in store, there was an untapped opportunity to bridge the digital and physical shopping experience. Trip planning, wayfinding, and shopping lists were the keys to making that connection.

Research

I brought users into the research lab and approached shoppers in store to understand how they were using technology while shopping. The key finding: while some used the default notes app on their phones, most still relied on paper and pencil.

Exploration

I mapped the user journey from building a shopping list at home to checkout and the return trip. I then sketched flows and created wireframes to show how Store Mode could work in parallel with the flagship app, calling out common controls and key differences to guide the development team.

Visual Design & Prototyping

I led a small team of visual designers to quickly create assets. We put these designs into a prototype and tested with shoppers at our local store, iterating rapidly to validate and improve the experience.

Impact

Stakeholders approved folding Store Mode into the flagship app. The addition of wayfinding and product availability increased shopping list adoption within the Target app.
